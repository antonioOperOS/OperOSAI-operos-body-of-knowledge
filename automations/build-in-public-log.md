## 2026-07-21 — FW-002 Leadership & High Output Management (partial)

**Milestone:** Migrated Leadership & High Output Management from stub to partial content — Grove, Horowitz, and Willink synthesized into a Decision Rights Matrix → Task-Relevant Maturity → One-on-One → Manager Leverage → Feedback Loop cascade, with an explicit RoT result metric for any AI-assisted leadership workflow.

**Antonio's voice (draft):**
> Rewrote our leadership doc today and caught myself running 1:1s wrong: mine had turned into status updates I ran, not an agenda my reports set. Grove's whole point is that a 1:1 doesn't produce output itself — it changes the quality of someone's work for the next two weeks. That's the actual leverage, not the meeting. Fixing my own calendar before I put this in front of anyone else.

**Rafael's voice (draft):**
> We defined "manager leverage" as team output change per hour of leadership time invested — not hours logged, not 1:1s held. Same rule we apply everywhere: if we ever automate 1:1 prep or blocker detection with AI, that automation doesn't count as done until it's tied to incremental revenue per token against a holdout. A leadership tool with no result metric is just another dashboard.

**OperOS company voice (draft):**
> Most "leadership frameworks" fail on one thing: they apply the same delegation style to every person and every task. The fix isn't a better meeting cadence, it's naming who decides what before the meeting happens, then matching delegation to actual skill per task, not seniority. We wrote our own Decision Rights Matrix before selling anyone else's. `FW-002` is live in the repo, marked `partial` on purpose — the parts we haven't proven yet aren't dressed up as done.
